ibuki

In 2017, I developed a wheel-driven android that could move with a human-like gait.

This gait mechanism was created by replicating the movement of a human’s center of gravity position – using a pair of eccentricity wheels for horizontal movement, and a linear actuator for vertical movement.

I also replicated human-like pelvis movements by using a gimbal-joint.

Balancing Robot

In 2016, we developed a super-light bipedal robot by using advanced materials: CFRP and UHPE wire.

Placing all motors on the trunk allowed the robot’s legs to move faster, and using the robot, we verified balance control based on ankle angular momentum.

This balance strategy maximized stabilizable regions, and could optimally switch balance controllers.

Optimal switching balance controllers involved recovering balance by using ankle torque, inclining trunk posture, and stepping.
